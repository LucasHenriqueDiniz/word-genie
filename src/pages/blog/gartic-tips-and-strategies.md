# How to Win More Gartic Games: Tips & Strategies

Winning consistently in Gartic comes down to three things: fast drawing, accurate guessing, and smart word selection. Master these fundamentals and you'll win way more games.

## Drawing Strategy

### Speed Over Perfection
A rough, recognizable drawing beats a beautiful, slow one every time. Aim to finish your key elements in 5-10 seconds.

### Focus on Silhouette
The overall shape matters most. Before adding details, make sure the outline is instantly recognizable.

### Use Color Strategically
Color communicates instantly:
- Red = apple, tomato, or danger
- Yellow = banana, sun, or happy
- Green = tree, grass, or plant

### Draw Distinctive Features First
Not all features matter equally:
- Cat: ears first (ears = cat)
- Giraffe: long neck first (neck = giraffe)
- Pizza: triangle first (triangle = pizza)

## Guessing Strategy

### Type Early and Often
Start typing partial guesses immediately. Don't wait for the drawing to be perfect.

### Look for Categories
Is it an animal? Food? Object? This narrows your guessing space dramatically.

### Use Context
Background elements matter:
- Water = fish, swimming, boat
- Trees = forest, nature, animal
- Stripes = zebra, tiger, or prison

### Common Wrong Guesses to Avoid
- Don't guess the drawing style, guess the object
- "Scribbles" probably means action, not a random object
- Stick figures are people doing things, not specific professions

## Word Selection Strategy

### Mix Difficulty Levels
- Start with easy words to get everyone involved
- Mid-game: mix in medium words
- End-game: challenge with hard words if you're winning

### Read Your Audience
- New players? Stick to easy categories like animals and food
- Experienced group? Challenge with complex words

### Know Your Strengths
- Great at drawing? Pick any word
- Great at guessing? Pick complex words so others struggle
- Balanced? Mix it up

## Round-by-Round Strategy

**Round 1:** Easy words. Let everyone warm up and get comfortable.

**Rounds 2-3:** Mix easy and medium. Build momentum.

**Rounds 4-6:** Introduce hard words strategically. Use them when you're winning.

**Final rounds:** All-out strategy. Pick words that help your team or hinder opponents.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Perfecting drawings** - Takes too long, second place in guessing  
❌ **Drawing too small** - Hard to see details  
❌ **Ignoring context** - Background elements help SO much  
❌ **Overcomplicating words** - Simple, recognizable is better  
❌ **Not using color** - Color is free and communicates instantly
